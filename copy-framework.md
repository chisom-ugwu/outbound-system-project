# Outreach Copy Framework & Guardrails

Our goal is to point out specific "lead leaks" to owners of local SMBs without sounding spammy.

### 🚫 Strict Guardrails
1. **No Em-Dashes:** Do not use `—` or similar long dashes. Use standard punctuation.
2. **No Quotation Marks:** Completely omit single or double quotation marks (`"`, `'`) inside the outreach emails.
3. **No Buzzwords:** Banned words include *streamline*, *synergize*, *disrupt*, *revolutionary*, *cutting-edge*, *innovative*, *optimum*, and *paradigm-shift*.

### ✉️ Cold Email Structure & Custom Offer
Each generated outreach message follows this strict 5-part layout:

1. **Subject Line (1 to 5 words):** Must mimic an internal communication style (e.g., `Quick question on lead response` or `missed connection check`).
2. **First Line (Personalization):** Business-level observation or trigger from their website/profile (e.g., *I noticed you are currently running Google Ads for HVAC services in Dallas*).
3. **Second Line (Problem Statement):** Specifically call out their lead response gap (e.g., *It looks like there is no option to text your team directly from the page, which means missed calls are likely going straight to voicemail*).
4. **Third Line (Value + Social Proof):** Point out the value of resolving this gap (e.g., *CoreAI helps local businesses automatically follow up with missed callers via SMS, turning up to 30 percent of missed calls into booked jobs*).
5. **CTA (The Best Offer - One-to-One Custom Audit):** End with a highly compelling custom opportunity assessment (e.g., *I put together a quick lead response audit comparing your site's response speed to 2 local competitors. Would you like me to send over the PDF?*).
